Brand Vision

At Campertje, we believe that camper travel is more than just a way to travel. Our vision is to make camper van life accessible and enjoyable for everyone, whether you‚Äôre a beginner or an experienced traveler. By offering educational content and a vibrant community, we aim to inspire and guide people toward unforgettable camper experiences.

Brand Name and Logo



As we wanted to make Campertje approachable for everyone, we used the form of (-tje), which gives the brand a warm and personal feel. It gives a sense of community.

The shapes and colors were carefully chosen to carry multiple meanings:

The Rising Sun
The arches in the logo resemble a rising sun on the horizon ‚Äì a universal symbol for the start of a new day and new adventures. This perfectly aligns with the feeling of freedom that comes with camper van travel.

The Wheel of a Camper (when flipped)
When you flip the logo upside down, it can also be seen as a camper wheel ‚Äì a symbol of motion, mobility, and the camper itself. This dual meaning reinforces the idea that Campertje is all about being on the road, literally and figuratively.


Brand Values

Educational, safety, community driven and adventurous. Knowledge makes your journey smoother. Weather it‚Äôs the safety rules of your camper van, planning a route, or finding the best camping to stay, we‚Äôre here to provide engaging content that helps travelers make informed decisions. Traveling should be adventurous, but never reckless. We educate our community on road safety, emergency preparedness, secure camping spots, and essential travel precautions to ensure a worry-free journey.

      • Marketing

      We are Campertje! A community-based platform where Dutch camper van lovers can share their tips and trips. Whether you are looking for a picturesque camping spot in nature or a handy solution for your overweight van, at Campertje, it is all about connection, safety and convenience on the road. In this section on marketing, we outline our marketing campaign and explain how we aim to create awareness for this platform.

      Campaign context and promotional activities

      The campaign is focused on positioning Campertje as a strong brand within the Dutch camper van community. We aim to raise awareness about the platform and encourage community engagement through social media and our website.

      What did we do?

      • Publishing reels and informative posts on Instagram and Facebook
      • Stories and polls to increase engagement
      • Referring to the upcoming landing page of the website, where readers can read more about the topic of the post

      On Instagram, we mainly focus on sharing informative posts and giving the viewer teasers of the blogs we upload on the website. We uploaded on Wednesday and Sunday at 12:00h lunchtime.

      We decided on this time, since our target audience mentioned in the interviews that they are mostly active on social media between 12 and 1 p.m. Furthermore, Wednesday is a typical break moment, and Sunday is ideal to inspire people with new plans or ideas for the upcoming week.

      For the post design, we tried to work within the frames of our style guide: orange and green as main colours, with a minimalistic and professional feel. All visual content fits the values of Campertje: awareness, safety and connection.

      Objectives

      All the campaign decisions that we made were based on our measurable goals tied to the planning and communication plan. The goal is to create more traffic to the website, where visitors can find blogs with more explanation about the social media posts and to build up a community within our target audience.

      • Reach objective: By the 1st of May, the following objectives should be reached:
        • 100 social media interactions (likes, comments, shares) on Instagram and Facebook combined.
        • At least 5 comments under posts on both platforms.
      • Affect objective: Creating a sense of community within our target audience, where users are stimulated to respond actively, share their experience and to participate in discussions about camper trips and safety.
      • Response objective: By the 1st of May, we expect to achieve:
        • To gain 100 followers in total on Instagram and Facebook in total.
        • An increase in traffic towards the website, where visitors can read in-depth blogs that are linked to the social media posts.

      Channels/Platforms

      For the execution of Campertje‚Äôs marketing campaign, we chose Facebook and Instagram as primary platforms. This decision fits the social media habits of our target audience and is supported through our research in the interviews and existing research.

      Facebook and Instagram are still belonging to the biggest social media platforms worldwide (Statista, 2024). Facebook offers many possibilities for building communities and interaction, while Instagram excels in visual storytelling and fast interaction. These insights fit perfectly with the vision that we want to convey throughout the marketing campaign of Campertje.

      Clarification

      Our target audience consists of Dutch camper van users in the age category between 25 to 60 years. We can separate this into two subsegments:

      • The older target audience (35-60 years) is very active on Facebook, particularly through community interactions, group activities and sharing their personal experiences.
      • The younger target audience (25-35 years) intensively uses Instagram for visual inspiration, short-form content and stories about travelling and outdoor lifestyle.

      Research shows that 70% of the Dutch Facebook users are active daily (Newcom, 2024). Furthermore, Instagram users spend 2 to 3 hours daily on the platform on average, whereby visual content such as reels and stories is mostly consumed.

      Interview social media insights

      In addition, we asked questions regarding social media usage to our camper van users that we interviewed. They gave us the following insights about their social media behaviour:

      • The most used media platforms within our interviews were Facebook and Instagram (respectively 89% and 76% of the interviewees).
      • The peak hours of social media consumption are in the evening (between 18:00 and 22:00).
      • The most important reason to consume social media is to get inspired and exchange experiences with others.

      Conclusion: Based on the existing market research and our interview insights about social media, we decided to use Facebook and Instagram as the main platforms for our campaign. Both channels offer exactly the tools that are required to generate traffic to the website and create an active community within our target audience.

      Takeaways

      During the Campertje marketing campaign, we gained several important insights that will help us further improve our marketing activities in the future.

      One of the most important lessons was that building a real community takes more time and continuous interaction than we expected. Although we post regularly, building engagement is still a process that requires more stimulation. In the future, we could focus more actively on provoking discussions and inviting users to share their personal camper experiences.

      It also turned out that static carousel posts (with multiple slides) performed better than expected, while reels, despite their general popularity, generated less interaction within our specific target group. This showed that our target group values practical tips and authentic content more than quick, trend-oriented videos.

      We also learned that the time of posting played a bigger role than we had anticipated. Posting on Instagram around 12:00 and on Facebook around 19:00 matched the peak activity of our target group well, according to the interviews and statistics.

      We also noticed that Facebook requires a different communication style than Instagram. Posts with more text and explanation performed better on Facebook, while Instagram responded better to visual and shorter updates.

      Finally, we discovered that hashtags are only effective if they are truly relevant and target group-oriented. General hashtags hardly contributed to additional reach; niche hashtags, specifically aimed at camper life, did generate new viewers.

      During the Campertje marketing campaign, we gained several important insights that will help us further improve our marketing activities in the future.

      Future planning

      If the marketing project for Campertje were to be continued, several points for improvement could be implemented based on the insights gained.

      The target group does not need to be changed. The focus on Dutch camper users between 25 and 60 years of age fits in perfectly with Campertje‚Äôs vision. However, we do recommend further in-depth research into their online behaviour, in particular into which type of content generates the most interaction and at what times they are most active. We already know that visual inspiration and practical tips work well, but more insight into the preferences for content formats (for example, short videos versus carousel posts) can further increase effectiveness.

      Another point for improvement is the communication and media plan. During the first campaign period, an average of four posts per week were posted across Instagram and Facebook. Although this provided a good basis, the frequency could be increased in a follow-up campaign. Social media platforms themselves advise posting more often for greater reach and higher engagement.

      A realistic goal would be to post four to five posts per week per channel, supplemented with regular stories to remain visible on a daily basis.

      In addition, we should use more interactive content, such as polls, quizzes and short questions in posts. This contributes to higher involvement and stimulates the community building that is central to Campertje.

      It is also an option to conduct a competition (for example, ‚Äúwin a camper travel kit‚Äù or ‚Äúshare your most beautiful camper spot and win a prize‚Äù). This can be a good method to quickly increase both reach and interaction.

      Finally, another point for improvement is to apply variation in content types even more consciously: alternate between inspiring photos, short videos, carousels, tips and personal stories. On Facebook, a little more emphasis can be placed on extensive posts with more text, while on Instagram, the visual experience remains central.

      Additionally, we advise setting new measurable objectives for the next campaign phase, so that we can build in clear evaluation moments and make adjustments where necessary.

      Problem
      • The overweight issue with campervans: Many campervans exceed legal weight limits, which reduces fuel efficiency, increases wear and tear, and can lead to fines.
      • Campervans too large for narrow roads: Concerns about road safety and route suitability. Traditional motorhomes struggle on narrow European roads and in remote locations, limiting accessibility.
      • No nearby repair services or technical support: When traveling, campervan users may not have access to garages, services, or technical help.
      • Resource management: Locating facilities for waste disposal and water replenishment is often a significant concern for travellers.
      Solution

      Because of these reasons, Campertje has developed a solution to address the challenges that travellers face on their adventures.

      • Providing consumers with educational advice and resources: Offer guides, tutorials (for operation and maintenance), a digital checklist (covering packing, setup, safety, and troubleshooting), and offer help to users.
      • Service Network: Establish partnerships with campsites and service providers, including a map of certified camper garages and support points.
      Customer segments

      Target audience: our camper lovers, aged 26-68. Those who are more financially stable and interested in sustainable travel. Families traveling with children or older adults. Values that drive out in adventures, education, community, and safety. However, there are also new campervan owners seeking guidance and community support.

      Unique value proposition

      Campertje has three key value propositions that make their brand and website unique:

      • All-in-one platform: For everything you need to know or want while traveling with your campervan.
      • Community engagement: We foster a supportive community where campers can share experiences, tips, and solutions.
      • Sustainable implementation: Promoting eco-friendly practices and solutions.
