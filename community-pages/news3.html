5 Common Mistakes You Might Be Making on Your Camper Trip

Nobody’s perfect — especially on the road. Whether you're a first-time vanlifer or a seasoned traveler, it’s easy to fall into habits that can turn your dream trip into a minor headache. The good news? These mistakes are totally avoidable once you know what to look out for.

1. Underestimating travel time

Google Maps might say three hours, but camper reality says five. Between mountain roads, scenic stops, and slower speeds, always give yourself extra time — and avoid the stress of rushing.

2. Not leveling your van

Sleeping on a slope sounds minor until your head is lower than your feet or your coffee slides off the table. A simple leveling block or a quick check with a spirit level app can save your sanity.

3. Forgetting to secure items before driving

One sharp turn and suddenly your kitchen becomes a mess. Before hitting the road, double-check that everything is stowed and locked in place — especially anything glass or heavy.

4. Skipping regular water checks

Running out of water is no fun when you’re in the middle of nowhere. Get in the habit of checking your water tank daily, and top up whenever you have the chance — even if you think you don’t need it yet.

5. Parking without checking the rules

Not every quiet spot is legal or safe to camp overnight. Always check local regulations or use apps and community tips to find reliable, allowed places to park.

Vanlife is all about freedom — but a bit of prep and awareness makes that freedom even better. Learn from the common missteps and you’ll enjoy the journey a whole lot more.
